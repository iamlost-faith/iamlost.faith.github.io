Hello there!

My name is Tatiana.
I have been a new born believer for 2 years now and counting. I was born into a Roman Catholic family, where I was a lukewarm christian (Believer but not practicant) for 19 years, became atheist at the age of 19 then became a new born again Christian seven years later.

I am happy you came here!

Here at my blog you will find stories to read, but also their meaning in the "Meaning" section on the story's page.
The aim of these stories is to edify already-believers but also to inspire non-believers or those falling away from faith.

How I converted (became a new born-again christian)


After seven years of atheism, a day dawns on me when all the rejections, pain and sufferings I had accumulated thus far find me howling on my guitar. Tears were pouring down my face like a left-opened tap and I just didn't know to whom to offload it all. So I started crying to someone out there. And although I didn't believe God existed at this time, the more I imagined He was listening, the more I cried.

Now how did I get there, I was an atheist to begin with.

So, my atheist days were composed of a lonely soul stealing and lying, for I hated justifying my truths, and was searching for meaning in anything that captured my attention.
I wanted to know how trees climbed upwards, how to create seeds too, why I couldn't be free in the literal sense of the word but was never questioning the practices of consuming thoughts from images and statues that had brought me to start praying to gods I started believing into existence, such as "the reason", "the indifference", "the universe". I had observed the world long enough and looked at specific images so much and consumed so many thoughts from them that I started believing they were a spirit behind them.

It wasn't like "because I don't have a god I may as well create my own", but more like "this image makes a specific sense to me, even in a way that I am reticent explaining to my family members, it speaks to my spirit and makes sense of my thoughts and keeps me company." And I remember a few days before crying on the guitar I was even praying to these "reasons" and "indifferences" that I had created. But not only praying to them felt wrong for some reasons, I had a flickering belief that if God didn't exist I would have created Him because I needed One in my life, and creating one was exactly what I had dedicated my last year of atheism doing without knowing I was.

The creating of my "god" started on a normal morning when my little sister and I had decided to go to IKEA to buy utensils for her new place. When rolling the cart, my eyes fell onto a wooden mannequin; the ones artists use for drawing references. Now these mannequins all had different poses but one in particular captured my attention for its posture gave it the impression of being "aware", in a sense. This caused it that everywhere I placed the mannequin, it would look as part of its surrounding, but also as part of itself. It was alive if you will, but only in my head. Everytime I would think, looking at it made my thoughts clearer. I still yet to know what this practice is called, but this time on the guitar though, I was tired of everything. I was tired of not being good enough at my job, I was tired of not being good enough for the guy I dearly loved, I was tired of having no one who understands me, I was tired of creating God because He didn't exist to me and I was tired of being a confusion to myself. I wasn't tired of denying any of this when asked and or stealing legos yet, but I was tired of controlling everything and was thirsting to understand the actual truth, to know the truth. So if a real God existed, it's to Him I wanted to believe I was crying this time around. And so I cried. And cried. And thought about my situation and cried some more.

And the more I was crying, the more I started crying into a melody that was like a howling in distress. I made sure all the pain was being portrayed, since this One "out-there" to me, didn't know just how hurt I was. Or so I thought. So to me, I was introducing myself. Then after a long time crying, an uplifting piece I had composed a while back came back to my mind, and my thoughts started singing it. Immediately I knew this was the answer: that I am pouring out sadness now, but everything will be okay in the end.

Now this didn't make me believe that the true God I was calling for was listening to me and had used a melody of mine as a response. No, not so fast! It didn't even give me the pride to think that I was the problem but also the solution since the howling was my voice and the song that had come was mine. What happened was I took it exactly how I felt: I am crying now, but I will be ok in the end. But "okay" according to me, was going to take a long time as it took a long time for the howling to turn into the uplifting piece.

Then exactly later this day, in the evening, I am standing on a busy road waiting for my family members to come out. As I wait, I make eye contact with a pastor driving on the road who pops his eyes out upon seeing me. Shortly he parks his car up ahead and I see someone coming back to speak to me. "What is your name?" he asked me. "Tati," I replied. He then looked into my eyes intensely as if urgently and says: "Say Jesus!". Obviously not knowing what Jesus is or "who" Jesus is anymore, there is no way I am saying "Jesus". Repeating three times, the outcome stayed the same on my side: silence. Eventually I burst and asked firmly "WHY ARE YOU COMMANDING ME TO SAY JESUS?". "I am not commanding you". He now says as if melting. "There was a prompt that entered me when I made eye contact with you, so I had to stop and speak with you, " he adds.

"A prompting?!" I now wonder, peeking at the side of the road. "That's something I am not aware happens to people" I add. "Could this be 'God's' answer to the cry I just had on the guitar before coming here?" I continued to wonder while the pastor was asking me questions I now remember nothing about. Eventually he asked for my phone number which I gave somehow, then we parted.

When the family reunion that brought me here ended, I was walking my bike to my sister's place where I was planning to sleep over for the weekend when I met a homeless man on the side of the road. Having food left over in my lunch box, I bent down from the bike I was pushing and asked him, "are you hungry?", "yes" he said. "I have some food", I said to him, opening the lunch box to him. But when he saw the food, he shook his head and said "no no". This puzzled me enough that when I met my sister I told her the story and it still puzzles me today I share it here once more. "If I were homeless and hungry," I now say to my sister, " I would eat anything!". "But do you know which hunger?" she replied. "So you can be hungry and have food, and won't eat because you have another type of hunger?!" I thought to myself. "The more you know!"

When she said that, I found that it was so deep, then I immediately looked into the oblivion in search for hungers I had that weren't healthy-food related at all. But honestly, I couldn't pick up what exactly the void inside of me was looking for. I knew I had been filling it with every hobby and bad practice I could allow myself to, I knew I had lived with it for so long it had now become a part of my way of being, but whether there was something that would pull me out of the gulf of despair, I don't think I would recognise it if someone stopped by to give it to me.

Now, despite being broken and exposed to second-hand smoking, I was strong enough not going the drug route. Now I believe thanks to God because I was an atheist still, but in reality, I may as well have been homeless and hungry and on the side of the road of existence and didn't even know I was. And funny thing, a pastor had also stopped by for me with the healthy-food for my hunger and I hadn't even realised. The irony. All I was busy wondering was "how come you are hungry and cold but you can be at least full and you are choosing not to."

I may have been hungry and cold and bare like the Laodicea church in Revelation 3:17 and didn't realise. All I was wondering was, again, "how come you are hungry and cold but you can be at least full and you are choosing not to."

Well, it's difficult to convince someone who doesn't feel cold that they are cold. Are you a Doctor or what? But this was going to be fixed very very soon. The next day, my sister goes to work and leaves the place to me. Alone with my thoughts. Now, one of the coping mechanisms I had of escaping the reality within was to bring the fight outside of me. I had become an extensive journalist and not the kind you watch on TV but the one who journals a lot because so much is just going on inside their mind. So after watching a video online of a panel that was planning to create a human in the laboratory I somehow protested against the practice when they said "they want to be first" and "make the buzz." Somehow I had replied, "everyone is here, and humans have been making themselves from the beginning of their existence, so YoU MuST Be LAsT!". As you can see, pushing down the prouds who will never read my journal was a job I had given to myself.

But just after writing that, dawned the existential question from an existential crisis that had been on-going long enough to become the status quo: "Then what if someone created me?" to which "my parents" didn't seem like a fitting answer. Anymore. Now both science and religion on this matter had one flaw, neither had a satisfactory proof of their claims. But the thing was also that, I never read anything about religion long enough to become interested except a fictional book on buddha which I threw away. Now as you can see, if God existed, He would have to unfortunately meet me at my lowest level; low enough I am sinking beneath the ground. Which He did sooner than I thought? Wait, am I that special?
From the pastor on the road, and the call out on the guitar, and the many questions I had with no answers whatsoever, my mind had become open and opened to something "out-there" without realising it. But immediately after asking myself the existential question above, the A4 page I was writing on spoke back to me. Now I will remember to add the exact things it said because I was journaling everything down. I was so surprised I told to myself "All this while I have been journaling, I didn't know the page could speak back!"

The page could speak back. Yeah. Right.

At this time, I knew when it was my own thoughts and I knew when it was not, because when "the page" spoke, I could answer in my own thoughts, and when I finished I knew I finished. Then it would continue. It was a voice inside my head, not through my ears. A-I-would-say masculine voice that was rather unhappy with me and that was condemning me. It seemed masculine because it had a timber of authority in it and it came in judgement.
Not one time it crossed my mind, "could this be...?" I had decided it was the page I was writing on that was speaking to me and that was it for me!

But ever since this "page" spoke to me, I was never the same. As I was writing the condemning, every single drop of contentment with my status quo started to leave me. More and more peace I started to crave. And more and more lost I started to feel. I then remember resting my fingers on the piano and realised I was now ok playing with a metronome. Something I was never ok with before this. My mind needed another sound to listen to, something else to follow; anything to make me not listen to these thoughts that are now ever-present even if I am not looking at the "page". At this stage I am no longer sure if it's the page at all.
Then the pastor from the day before called me. I let the phone ring to its completion because I just wasn't in the mood to pick up. But when he hung up, a question came inside my mind. "How do you call an unanswered prayer?", to which I realised I didn't know what to realise, I replied: "a missed call?". Terrified by what I had just said, "what if I pray to God and He doesn't pick up the call? On purpose? Because He is not in the mood? How would that feel?" I thought to myself.

So... did I start to know that God existed but I couldn't just accept Him in my life. Is this what was happening? Am I a new born-again christian now?

When my sister came back, I quickly used her internet to buy phone credits. When she left the place again, I myself called the pastor. I remember the phone call started with a "Sorry I didn't pick up your call." The rest of the call I remember I was quietly listening to him telling me many things about Jesus that I also forgot except, except, "He is the only way. The only way to end war, the only way to peace." When he mentioned "the only way to peace", this resonated with me to a deeper level because world peace was something I cared a lot about. Now that I think about it, my own peace was now in shambles but I cared about world peace. The irony.

If there is one thing I learnt from the psychiatrist afterwards, it was that "sometimes, the problem to fix is me, first of all." Around this time, something strange was happening to me. When people would express themselves through words or even body language, if they said something painful that happened to them, I would feel the pain transferred inside my chest like an infusion of resentment. The same way images were speaking to my mind, people's words were speaking to my body. I was as if "consuming" people's pains. But also joys. But also experiences. I was who some people call "a sponge". It had become too much at some point, than I started losing my name and started de-identifying to the place where I was from because it seemed to me, I was from everywhere I have lived, and from everywhere the people I spoke to have lived, and names didn't make sense to me any longer because inside of me, I carried the emotions transferred from so many people with different names. But for whoever cared to ask, "Tati" was the filler, when "Tatiana" didn't make any sense either.

But this situation started much earlier. It was when I moved to Ireland and decided after only 2 weeks of watching TV to stop watching the news. There was so much pain going on in the world, and I believe there still is. Watching the news for me was like drinking pain directly from the ocean. I became an atheist around this time, not because of the question, "if God exists then why is there so much suffering in the world?" but rather its answer.

Now, I was not a saint during this time. In fact, one of my biggest sins I am too ashamed to talk about was happening at this time. Besides, I was doing many other things I am was not proud of on Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday and go about refugee myself in the church for roughly one hour every Sunday, then come back to my despicable life Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, you get the idea.

But this one Easter Sunday 2015, was different for me. I wake up, put on my trousers (because back then I used to wear trousers), and go to church, it's Easter come on! I also went to church with nothing in my pocket, because I never really understood the concept of giving money to God. But as the church starts and the church organ starts to play, I start to cry.
Suddenly after this, the middle of my chest starts to burn. Then what felt like an outburst of pain started to extrude the middle of my chest to fill my whole chest. This was so painful that gallons and gallons of tears were pouring off my eyes. "What's going on?" I now wonder while crying. The basket of offering passing by me, I pick it up and pass it to the next person. When the communion started I felt unworthy to approach it because I felt maybe all the things I was doing Monday Tuesday Wednesday Thursday Friday Saturday had disqualified me from approaching the table. So immediately I concluded inside my mind, with my own voice, "God is kicking me out of church." And this is how I left the church, lost my faith one hour later, grew agnostic as the years passed, then an atheist afterwards.

The very pain that gets people closer to God, separated me from him.

The next time I would feel a pain so atrocious again would be exactly three days after calling upon the name of Jesus, but this time, instead of the pain extruding my chest, the pain was intruding. Imagine someone taking a rose thorn and piercing it slowly all the way into your chest. That's how it felt.
I still yet have to know the meaning of this pain but one thing it didn't do this time around was separating me from Jesus, who I had just found. Or rather to be precise, who had just found me. All I was thinking of was recognising the pain was there and trying to ignore it as best as I could.

Over the years, and after cutting off the news for so long, one thing I realised didn't change, was the ability to consume feelings. I could cut off the news; which surprised my University Lecturer when he found out I knew nothing about Trump's tweets, but there were still people around me. When "pages" started talking back to me after all the world problems I journaled on them, that's when I realised I can care so much about the imbalance of the things in the world and carry the pain of the whole world in my very body, but when I start to experience heat inside my chest, the one needing the war to end within is not the world first of all, it's me. So when I heard that Jesus was the ONLY way to world peace, that was something I felt off-loaded off me and for the first time, I felt I had something in common with Jesus. But did this turned me into a new born-again believer? Erm... not yet.

In fact, when the weekend ended, I remember going back to my condemned ways of living despite recognising that the "page" was condemning me righteously. "Why would I listen to a page?" I had told myself.

It's until three weeks later, when I came back to my little sister's place again, that not just the page, but everything started to speak back to me. I just had to look at it, and a thought would come inside my mind. I remember this day I was so hungry because I had spent the whole day journaling. When I picked up my bike to go to my sister's place, I walked it again because this time, I really couldn't cycle with all that hunger going on.

Now, before leaving my house, there was a flickering light that I saw through my window. It was so bright it captured all my attention. It didn't seem like an aeroplane because it was fixed inside the sky. It also didn't seem like a star because it was too low in the sky. It was more like a low hanging star but also an aeroplane without making much sense of one or the other. I tried to take a picture of it but at the same time I didn't want to lose sight of it because it captivated me so much. And because everything I was looking at was speaking to me, when I looked at it, the thing it said was "let me bring you home."

So... was the place where I was living "a house" and my sister's place, "a home"? What did it really mean by that? And because I hadn't eaten all day, and had no food in my pantry, I decided "home is where food was".
A hectic walk then followed to my sister's place where I met all kinds of people including the Police asking me if I was okay, and another homeless person telling me "I hope you are okay, you look sad." Which I found odd because I thought "I am very much okay, and you are probably going through much worse than I am but you are asking me if I am okay?! Hǎoxiào!"
Now, the reason I looked sad was because my mind was wondering why homeless people exist. "Is it a fault in the 'system', is there something I am missing?" Before this homeless man who told me I looked sad, and just before the police asked me if I was okay, I had met a homeless woman who after speaking to, I didn't quite understand why she was homeless, hence "why homelessness on the street?" Being tired and hungry, I also didn't even know I had any money on me at all until I searched my bag nonetheless. Head down and puzzled, I met the police just after hugging her and the next homeless man who asked me whether I was okay not far from here. I gaze at him and realise I had given all the money to the first homeless person and now dread the thought that "I can't help them all". This saddened me so much than when he asked me whether I was okay it saddened me even more. "Why can't the whole world be okay?" then became the next train of thoughts. Now you know that every problem in the world used to be my problem. And let's not mention the need for organic salmon because of sea-pollution and objects from Amazon and Aliexpress that claimed to work in a certain way but failed to.

But the head-turning part was when I reached the road to my sister's place. Waiting to walk the traffic light, I raise my head to a full moon with a rainbow ring on it. So bright it makes me feel bad about the flicker of light from before. "The moon is always here, but I don't always notice it. Yet a flicker of light appears one day and captures all of my attention." I thought to myself. "What if God is the same?" I now wonder. "What if He is always there and I don't notice Him, but when I saw a carved mannequin of a specific expression it captured all of my attention!"

But this comparison, even if apparent, did not make me a new born-again christian. But it now went without saying that the glass of my atheism now had a shatter in it. During the three weeks when I had gone back to my old self, I had talked to another pastor I knew from back home and told him about the pastor on the road. But the angry tone in his voice didn't make me feel welcome in God's home so again, I didn't become a new born christian. Imagine a lost soul comes to you telling you about an experience that confuses them and all you can do is shout at them "one day you will see that God is real" "You see yourself as a little god you who think you have it all sorted." Obviously what they will do after this conversation is run. But I didn't. I kept listening without retaliating, keeping my confusion to myself as it seemed he wasn't open to listening to it. Although he mentioned "how would you know God wasn't speaking to you through that pastor", the way he said it made me feel inadequate. So from this, I absolutely did not become a new born-again christian.
But there was one thing he said that resonated with me. Having English as my third language, he said, "You are saying that you didn't say 'Jesus' because what the pastor said seemed to come to you from out of nowhere?! take english. Before you learnt English, didn't it all seem dark to you? But the more you learnt the language, the more its meaning revealed itself to you. God is the same. At first He makes no sense. But as you build a relationship with Him, you realise He is not that foreign anymore." I had never seen it this way. He had a point. And Although he is my mentor today, the way this conversation had been going thus far, actually turned me away from becoming a born-again christian.

Now it seems that to be born at all one needs a seed while everything and everyone had been the incubator but the seed itself was still missing. Luckily because everything I was looking at was speaking to me at this time. Arriving at my sister's place a sticky note she left on the piano that read "Food (prayer)" also made a specific sense to me, as prayer seemed to be a food for those with a specific hunger for it. But was I one of them?
The way I had made sense of "let me bring you home" from the flickering light was "home was where food was" and here I was arriving at nothing but a sticky note telling me "Food(prayer)". Obviously there is no way I was going to pray. But the sticky note was so timely it made me ask my little sister, who was a believer, a question that stirred the conversation into why the devil is the way he is, and which somehow I understood, because not only everything I would look at had meaning to me, everything I would hear also created a different meaning. When she presented food to me, I told myself, "I want to stay in this state" so I slept without eating actual food because I thought maybe it's because I accidently fasted for the whole day that everything I looked at spoke to me. But was this true? I have fasted for three days more than once now, and nothing of the sort has replicated itself. So, I am not entirely sure!

But it was during this very night that I didn't know things would change. First of all I go take a shower and with the loudness inside my mind, I tell myself "I can't go on living like this". Was I hungry? Very much so, of which food, I didn't know. So I started to "inco-locate". A practice I had made sense of in my own ways in which my thoughts scream inside my mind calling not for someone "out-there" but for someone "in-there" since they always say "search inside yourself", right?
Thinking it would make any difference, nothing but only frustration replied. Then I remembered praying to "the indifference" then "the reason" to which nothing replied. Then I concluded it was futile to pray.

But one thing I haven't said, was my rather weird naming conventions. For corners in my rooms, I remember giving them names like , "at thoughts", "at things", "at words" instead of calling them "over the table", "over the table by the window" or "in the closet". Why? because I had created a replication of my mind inside, outside in my room, the same way I was journaling to bring out the fights from within.
But consequently, this naming convention matched a Catholic prayer; the confession prayer. When I remembered they matched, I started reciting the prayer to see what else was inside to inspire me.

This was the same day I had done the cry on the guitar and after reciting the confession prayer not because I meant it, I also recited the "our Father" prayer, just to see what was inside of it. And this is also the same day I met the pastor. But did any of this make me a new born-again christian? Nope! I remember that night when inco-locating, it must have been some sort of prayer, because I was calling again for something inside of me, anywhere! To come and save me...from myself. But nothing replied. Then in the morning, after having a very vivid dream of seeing some kids I knew in completely white bodies but one of the kids in their families in the dark, a tangible fear started chasing me. I don't think it was related to the dream but all of the sudden, around 5am, I started to shake. At first murmuring that someone is out to get me, while feeling something dark inside of me, to actually becoming agitated and waking my little sister up. Then the fear amplified ten folds, then a hundred folds, when it started reaching thousand folds I remembered the pastor on the road and started screaming "Jesus” as loud as I could allow myself. "Jesus Jesus Jesus Jesus" I started screaming repeatedly, now shaking because the fear was so tangible and so strong it was giving me nausea. And just when I was there panting and screaming Jesus's name, peace instilled itself inside of me.
This peace was so pure; almost ethereal.
It had a presence to it and made all the fear go away.

Sitting in presence of it, it was as if Jesus himself was there. So I stayed quiet recollecting myself from what had just happened. Here was when I told myself, "it's real!", "Jesus is real". And from this day onwards, I had become a new-born again christian. Finally!
But it didn't end here.

That morning too, everything I looked at was still speaking to me. So after removing almost all the artworks inside of her place that were saying mean things, I picked up my sister's bible. I didn't know where exactly to read, so I would open the bible and whichever page I landed on, I would content myself with what it would say to me. Then at eleven in the morning as I read the bible out loud on the window, what felt like golden sand filled the inside of my forearms only, at first. Now this was almost the opposite of the feeling of fear I had experienced earlier in the morning. Not knowing what it was, I simply said "what usually happens to people in Africa is happening to me too", because there I saw people fall under the anointing, but it had never happened to be before. Shortly after, the "golden sands" expanded in my whole body like light. And I could feel it from head to toe. It wasn't sore, but it was tingling strongly. Still not knowing what to do with it, I continued to read the bible. Then it stayed with me for five whole minutes, then it went away.

When later on I explained to my mom about it she said "that was the Holy Spirit!", to which I was surprised and retorted my eyes poked out "it was??". I then remember so badly wanting Him to come back again so I can tell Him I recognise Him now, but you can only have the baptism of the Holy Spirit once. And this is how I became truly a new-born again christian, because the days that followed this day, I realised I had become unable to do the bad practices from before. Everything from lying, stealing, etc. was not part of my identity anymore. I had picked up the bible and was happy to live by its teachings.

So, was Jesus a hunger I had? When peace came to fill the void inside, I knew at least, He was the answer I needed.

I now also know there are so many ways to pray and sometimes we pray without even knowing we are. But also, it's only when you pray to something real that you get anything back. Knowing who was speaking through who and through what because they are already in everything or anyone was the challenge.

But I am glad I caught the meaning, that God is with me at all times.
