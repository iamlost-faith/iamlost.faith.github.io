> Not a hill to climb.

There was a time when a sheep and a tiger were great friends. The tiger had one cub, and he loved it dearly.<br>
There was also a tortoise they would go to everytime they needed advice.<br>

One day the tiger went to hunt and left his cub with the sheep. Then many hours passed.<br>When the sheep noticed that the tiger, this time, took longer than usual, the sheep took the cub and ate it, leaving only bones.<br>
Not knowing what to do with himself, the sheep decided to run to the tortoise and ask for advice.

Arriving at the tortoise's, the sheep confesses:<br>
"Tortoise!" he bleats, "I... I have just eaten the tiger's cub". It said. "You, a sheep, ate the tiger's cub?". The tortoise retorts. "Yes, me." The sheep answered. "Really YOU?" The tortoise adds peering at the sheep with a single eye. "Yes, 'I'." He confirmed. "And now if the tiger comes back, I don't know what he will do to me." The sheep added <!--it's voice trailing off-->.<br>
"Well." The tortoise said. "I can advice you." The tortoise continued. "But those I advice never listen." The tortoise added.
"Give me the advice, I am in great need... I will listen." The sheep begged.<br>

Meanwhile at the house, the tiger returns to find an unsettling silence; seeing neither sheep, nor cub, but only bones. Immediately it concludes that the sheep ate its cub, because he left the cub to the sheep.<br>
Consumed by rage on top of not knowing what to do, the tiger decides to go to the tortoise to ask for advice.

Arriving at the tortoise's, the earth trembled and the air crackled as the tiger jumped into the enclosure. Claws fully unsheathed, a storm of grief and rage swirled around him ready to shear the sheep. His grief was great than to say "he was a red-hot iron seeking to plung into water" would be an understatement.
"Tortoise!" he roared, the sound echoing through the trees. "The sheep ate my child! I need to find him and kill him!"
 "He ate your child?" the tortoise exclaimed with as if hearing the news for the first time. "Yes!" the tiger thundered. "I left him in the sheep's care, and all I found were bones! Advise me on how I can find that him please." The tiger added.<br>
 "Hmm," the tortoise mused, extending his neck for a better look at the agitated tiger. 
 "Are you hearing yourself?" the tortoise asked. "Yes". The tiger answered. "And those who are not here, are you hearing him?" The tortoise checked. And silence answered.

"Mr. Tiger," the tortoise said, "I can offer you advice. But those I advice never listen."
 "I will listen" The tiger promised. "You will listen?" The tortoise checked. "Yes." The tiger confirmed. "And those who are not here, are you hearing him?". And silence answered once more.

Then the tortoise checked with the tiger, "You want to know where to find the sheep so you can kill him, aren't you?", "Yes!!" The tiger replied.
"You know the things he likes?" The tortoise asked. "Yes, grass, and leaves." The tiger replied. "Do you know where he likes to eat it?" The tortoise asked. "Yes, at that hill with a tree not far from the rocky mountain." The tiger replied. <br>
"Now go wait for him on that hill." The tortoise finished.
"Thank you tortoise", the tiger said, then he left.

Now the tortoise took the sheep out from behind the curtains where he was hiding him. "Did you hear that?" The tortoise asked, "Yes, I heard." The sheep said. "Did you hear for real?", "Yes" the sheep insisted.<br>
"What excatly did you hear?" the tortoise checked. "I like grass and leaves." the sheep answered. "And?" the tortoise added. "And the tiger wants to find me and kill me." the sheep replied. "Andddd????" The tortoise angrily asked as it now seemed that the sheep was missing the whole point. "And the tiger went to wait for me on that hill I frequent to kill me."<br>

"Perfect! Now my advice to you is, don't ever go on that hill ever again." The tortoise said.
"No I won't." the sheep retorted. "I definitely won't." It added, at the worrisome gaze of the tortoise. He then shortly left, taking the opposite direction away the hill.


Weeks passed. Then munching on grass somewhere far away from the forbidden hill, the sheep started talking to himself. It said: "The grass at that hill used to taste better." it started. "Grass these days doesn't taste as good as they used to." It continued.
- "Hmm.. I wonder what the tiger is up to".<br>
- "Surely all this time," 
- "I mean, do you mean to tell me he would wait years just for me?" 

He continued speaking to himself watching himself advancing closer and closer to the forbidden hill claiming each next grass to be better than the previous... "This can't all be in my mind. The grass on this side of the hill is certainly better" he added. 

But is the way that the sheep thought the same way as the tiger thought?

Arriving not far from the foot of the hill, he inspected for the presence of the tiger and saw nothing. Then immediately started munching on the grass at the foot of the hill going upwards to what its psychology judged to be better and better grass.

The tiger patiently waiting in the tree for forever now,

Startled,
![img](/img/posts/2024-3-1-Not-A-Hill-To-Climb/startled.jpg)
It positioned himself and waited for the right moment.
![img](/img/posts/2024-3-1-Not-A-Hill-To-Climb/strategising.jpg)

Then as soon as the sheep got close enough, the inevitable happened.<br>

Leaving only bones, the tiger went back to the tortoise and told him: "You are the best advice giver there is, the best there will ever be."<br>
"In the same way you advised me to go and wait for the sheep at the hill, the same way I did and lo and behold it came and I killed it!" The tiger added. "Thank you tortoise, you are the best of the very best advice givers."<br>

Touched by the misfortune of the sheep, the tortoise admitted: "Do you know, that very same day you came to ask for advice the sheep was here inside my house listening? He was right here!". 

The tiger too happy to ask the tortoise why it hid the sheep from him, went back to his home dancing.

The end.

## Meaning

> The tortoise can stop a raging tiger, but we have to stop ourselves.

The first thing my dad said upon hearing the story was "well, but sheeps don't eat meat!",  "Well, do christians tell lies?"<br>

The tiger wouldn't go after the sheep i it hadn't eaten its cub.
The devil can't come after us, if we didn't do what's his.

We have the bible at our fingertips full of advice and wisdom. When it's not enough, prayers and revelations are there. But how long do they last before we forget them and find ourselves again climbing hills we shouldn't?<br>

For example:<br>
- Sexual sin is not a hill for a follower of Christ to find themselves on.<br>
- Worshipping idols is not a hill for a follower of christ to find themselves on.<br>
- Stealing is not a hill for a follower of Christ to find themselves on.<br>
- Murdering/Suicide is not a hill for a follower of Christ to find themselves on.<br>
- Unbelief is not a hill for a follower of Christ to find themselves on.<br>
- 

Why? because we don't look like wind to the devil when we frequent such mountains, those of the devil do. Us, we look like sheep.

When you are at your lowest after doing something wrong and you run to God, he hides you from your enemies. He won't directly deliver you in the hands of the devil for doing wrong after you have run to him in repentance. It's written `He who dwells in the secret place of the Most High shall abide under the shadow of the Almighty.
 I will say of the Lord, “He is my refuge and my fortress..."`<br>

> But should He blame the devil if he finds you on a hill He told the devil to wait for you on and told you never to climb?

Does the devil forget or get tired of waiting?<br>
If not, then why should grass be greener for us only at the forbidden hills?
