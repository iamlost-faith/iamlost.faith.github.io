> Until you look.

There was a homeless white mouse that lived in a town far away.<br>
Everyday it waited on the street with its hands stretched begging for something. anything!<br>
To live longer or to die young, wondering was a luxury too pricey for him. Making it through each day was enough.

One evening, a rabbit passes by. Watching the mouse's hand trembling with tiredness, it approaches it looking at it compassionately. Seeing its suffering, the rabbit reaches inside its coat, takes the one thing it had in it and gives it to the mouse.<br>

The mouse, seeing that somebody stopped by to stare even longer than 2 seconds, took what the rabbit gave him without losing eye contact with him, ever-so-slightly lowering its head to placed it in its pocket then started to plead. "Please help me", "I need food", "I need clothes", "I want to stay alive"... The rabbit, puzzled, tells the white mouse frankly: "What I gave you is all I had.", But the mouse refused to believe it by insisting even more.<br>
"Please help me." "Please buy me food", "Please give me clothes"... Hearing this, the rabbit's head started filling itself with many questions: "how much should I have given?" "Why is everything I had not enough?", and it even wondered whether the mouse was being ungrateful. "My home got taken away from me" The mouse continues. "My daughter was taken away from me. Please help me." The mouse insists. Then the rabbit, not knowing what more to do, what else to say, started walking away. But at each step, the mouse followed pleading and pleading. "Please buy me food.." the mouse insisted, "You can buy food with what I gave you" the rabbit replies. "Please buy me shoes." "She can buy clothes too with what I gave her, why is she asking?" the rabbit wonders. "Do you have spare clothes?" "Please help me." Finally the rabbit realised and asked: "Have you looked at what I gave you?".<br>

When the mouse finally looked, it couldn't believe its eyes. His face finally glowed with awe. "Thank you!" "Thank you!" it now shouts. "Thank you sooo much" he now says to the rabbit, as it hugs it emotionally.

The end.


# Meaning

All this while, the mouse thought that the rabbit had given what people usually give to him: cents, scraps and crumbs. Therefore, it didn't bother to look.

Similarly, we won't realise the gift of salvation, until we look.

None of us is the rabbit. We are all homeless on this earth. We ask life for food, ask life for clothes, ask life to stay alive, etc.

But more than this has been given to us, yet, we failed to: realise.

**Jesus is thanked by those who understand the value of the cross.**
