> The One Thing.

There were two young people growing together who used to be great lovers. They were very close and loved each other so much that the girl started telling herself that this is the guy she would marry when she'd get older.<br>

Then one day, the guy moved to a different city. There, he met another girl and married her.<br>
Not very long after this marriage, the guy and the girl from before met again. "It's been a long time!" The girl exclaimed. "Yes, it has...", The boy, who was now a man, replied. "What have you been up to?" The boy added. "Just the usual course of life... I finished my studies and now work. Yourself?", the girl asked. "Cool! You haven't gotten married yet?" The boy asked. "No, not yet." The girl replied. "I had vowed to myself that I would marry you." She added. Upon hearing this the boy did not know what to say. So he spoke his truth. "I got married already. But if you've been waiting for me, I can divorce with my wife and marry you instead."

After this conversation ended, the boy went back to his home and started insulting his wife. After a week of insults, he had picked up drinking and when he saw that the wife was not leaving him on her own, he decided to initiate the divorce. "Look. You can choose to take everything you want that I own." The man started. "You know where my car is, you know how much I have in my bank account, you know where I keep the documents for my house, if there is any artwork you particularly like, you can take it via the divorce" the man continued. "but you can't take my company for I need to survive too, but if you want 49% of it, you can have that too." He finished. "Choose now what will become yours after we part, and be gone back to your family." He added.

Upon hearing this, you'd think the spoil of choice would be enough to unsink the wife's heart, but everything he mentioned comma after comma seemed to her heart like withering leaves. The wife loved the husband so much that she contemplated suicide.<br>
After days of not deciding what to take, the boy had come back home late from drinking. Upon seeing him, she ran in the room and picked up a duvet. She then wrapped the man inside of it, called a taxi and brought him to her family.<br>
When they arrived, he arranged a room for him and got him to sleep. The next day, the man did not know how he got there. So he went to interrogate whoever he would meet first in the house. "How did I get here?" He shouted at the girl's mom. Hearing this, the girl's mom went to search for the girl and left them. 

Question after question, the wife stayed quiet because the boy was almost boiling. "You are not serious." The boy uttered. "How would you convince me to come here?" "Answer me!!!"
When he pressed her to speak, the girl couldn't keeping him wondering any longer and broke the silence. "You told me to bring home with me everything I wanted before we'd part." The girl said. "Yesterday when you came back, even if at your lowest again, I realised you only in everything you own used to bring me great joy. That's why I wrapped you and brought you home with me."

Upon hearing this, the boy dropped to his knees.<br>
And started to cry.
"You'd still choose me, after all the insults and my bad behaviours?"<br>
When he finished crying, he confessed what had happened with the other girl and why he was behaving this way.

They then stayed married, and he forgot about the other girl.

The end.

## Meaning

We get courted all the time in our lives about Jesus.<br>But we choose not to go with who will love us, but with what we are used to.<br>

> The girl from before represent his old religion/habbits. The girl he married now, represents Jesus.

Someone may be used to habits **or way of thinking from before**, but it's the Jesus you are hearing of now that will love you. Choose you. Die for you.

And some even when they are with Jesus, they would leave him for something that wouldn't love them as much. An addiction, a secret sin...[the list is exhausive]

But those who have truly experienced the Joy in Christ Jesus, being offered anything else but him is torment. You just feel it inside of you that something existed, now it's missing. Or miss something you never had.

Some of us may think: I have hated Jesus, I have insulted Him in my heart. I have done this, I have done that and there is no way Jesus can accept me. Do you want me to share with you how the bible defines love? `Love is patient and kind. Love is not jealous or boastful or proud or rude. It does not demand its own way. It is not irritable, and it keeps no record of being wronged. It does not rejoice about injustice but rejoices whenever the truth wins out. Love never gives up, never loses faith, is always hopeful, and endures through every circumstance.`

I want you to keep in mind, `it keeps no record of being wronged` and `endures through every circumstance`.

Jesus was given all the kingdom of the world and their splendor, but he refused. `Mathiew 4:8-10`, But when it came the time to choose us, despite the fear of death, he did it.`John 19:30`

But like in that online video in which people get asked to choose between 1 billion dollars now, or an eternity with Jesus, decision is yours too.

The question is: are you ready to be loved?
