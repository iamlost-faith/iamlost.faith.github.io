> Nothing To Hide

One day, a hunter and its dog decided to hunt down a leopard that was killing people.
The hunter's dog was trained on many animals and today, a leopard's skin had been shown and sniffed to it.

Arriving at the hunting site, the hunter keeps an eye on its dog, for wherever the dog senses, there the leopard should be.

When the dog started being agitated, the hunter patrolled the area and saw indeed a leopard yonder.
He then started sneaking toward it to ensure a gunshot would be within its range.

But the leopard, hearing the noise from the dry leaves coming from the dog’s indiscrete walking, became suspicious and started to run.

When the hunter saw this, he gave up on sneaking and shut the gun in the direction of the leopard, following the leopard together with its dog.

When the leopard realised that it was indeed being hunted down it started cornering around fields.

Because the leopard was very fast, it had gone far enough to a place where it met a farmer ploughing in a field.

"Please help me", the leopard says to the farmer. "Someone is trying to hunt me down." the leopard added.

"Please help!" the leopard insisted.

Without questioning, the farmer instructed, "Go into the leafy pit there, I will cover you with sticks and leaves on top."

Quickly the farmer hid the leopard and covered the top of the pit with sticks and leaves, but as he started adding soil on top of it as fast as he could, the hunter entered the scene.

"Did you see a leopard pass here?" the hunter asks the farmer.

"A leopard?" The farmer replies.

As the hunter explains to the farmer how the leopard looked, the dog starts barking by the pit.

When both looked at the dog not just barking but also going in circles around the pit, the hunter looked at the farmer. "Are you sure you didn't see a leopard coming here?", but everytime the farmer denied, and now even more firmly. "I told you already!"

“This leopard is killing people,” the hunter explained. “If you know where it is, please!” But the hunter replied “I have never seen a leopard in my entire life!”, as the dog continued to bark even louder and louder by the pit.

The hunter called its dog that now didn’t want to leave the pit area and left.

Two times, the hunter looked behind his shoulder at the farmer to see if he would come to his senses and admit he was hiding the leopard, but the farmer kept his straight face.
When he was now far enough, the farmer called the leopard out saying: "hey mate, the person who was looking for you has left."

The leopard leaving the pit replied "the person?" then looked at the farmer as if seeing him for the first time, "who are you?" the leopard then asked the farmer. Now remembering *"it's killing people"*, the farmer started to tremble. "Are you not a person?" the leopard continued. "No, I am the one who saved you." the farmer justified.
"But are YOU not a person?", then before the farmer even replied, the leopard leaped on him and attacked him.

Now the farmer starts to scream. "Hunterrr, the leopard you were searching for is here", but the hunter despite hearing, didn't even look back.

"Hunterrr!!!!" The farmer now screams bleeding and being killed.

The end.

## Meaning

> Sin have found a home inside our hearts.

The hunter here represents the preacher. The dog that's trained on many prays represents the bible. The farmer represents someone who met with sin but didn't know it was sin but when they were told it was, they did not abandon it. Eventually, they died in their sins.

Take an example: many of us must have met with many kinds of sins when we were at various beliefs in our lives.
Let say Jesus decided to target sexual sin for example, and asked us to confess it so it might be killed, how many would hide it?<br>
How many would admit, "I am hiding it. It came to me x day and ever since..."<br>
Or many will say, "I know it's a sin but I can't abandon it", or straight on say "it's not a sin."<br>

Reflect on that a bit.

The leopard is hunting **everyone**. Not just the one hunting it. It doesn't matter if you are young, old, sick, healthy, sin is hunting down everyone. Not just ignorants. Even hunters can fall into temptation.<br>
In real-life it's possible that preachers also hide sin while pointing at sin using even the Word of God. If this is the case, do what Jesus said `So you must be careful to do everything they tell you. But do not do what they do, for they do not practice what they preach.`  **`Mathew 23:3`**

Another example is theft. Theft is a sin hunting down everyone. When you become a thief, theft is happy. It lures you into thinking you will never get caught. Preachers telling you stealing kills, get you to turn a deaf ear. The day that you will steal and not get caught, theft will tell you “you are the best.”, But the day that you will steal and get caught, theft will tell you “I never knew you” and the preacher will not be there to save you. Theft will even suggest that you get severely punished, and it will make sure it brings guilt with it.<br>

Thus, theft is not your friend. And neither is any other sin.<br>

But likely we are free from guilt and shame, by the work Jesus did on the cross. `1 John 1:9`<br>
But does this mean we should go on and sin as we wish because we can repent later (making God's grace abound?), `Romans 6:2` says: "Of course not."

The story is not to confuse, however; hunters are not hunting down farmers. They are hunting down the leopard that the farmers are hiding.<br>
Similarly, preachers are not pointing at sinners, they are pointing at the sin that the sinners are practising.<br>

God doesn't hate sinners, God hates sin. `Isaiah 59: 1-2`<br>

In conclusion, follow what the bible is screaming is sin, and abandon it. `Matthew 7:24`<br>
The wage is sin is death. `Romans 6:23`
