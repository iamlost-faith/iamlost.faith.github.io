> I came from way too far to fall.

There was a king in a kingdom far away who had one princess.<br>
This princess was in the age to get married but had not yet chosen with whom.
Addressing the request to her father, her father, the king, decided to find someone for her.

Now, both the king and the princess were very wealthy and lacked nothing. They could literally buy any other kingdom they wished.
So, to find a suitable husband, the king decided to organise a contest in which each participant would race against the princess, and proclaim as husband the one who will win against her.

The first man came. Someone very fit and handsome.<br>
At the sound of the bell, the race started. But just when the man made the first step, the princess threw money onto the ground. When the fit and handsome man saw this, he started amassing money, searching for the highest notes and lump sums first. "Ah, I will catch up with her anytime", the man said to himself. Then continued to amass some more. At this, the princess run further.<br>
As soon as he was about to reach her again, the princess threw even more money and the man reached out for the cash again picking up the lump sums as these were thicker this time. Five times the princess repeated this process, the 6th time, when she saw she was much closer from the finish line, and had no money left, she removed her crown and threw it further back. The man racing backwards for the crown, the princess reached for the finished line and won the race.

When the man lost the race, they took all the money he had picked up away from him telling him "you can't take these because these belong to the game."

This is how the first man went empty-handed, losing both the money and the princess.<br>
The princess then wore back her crown and prepared herself to start over again with the next man.<br>
Will this one be the one?

When all the potential men of the kingdom had passed and failed the race, a leper came.

At first the arbiters didn't want to let him race at all.
The king, watching the dispute, settled it saying, "I didn't give any other requirements to marrying my daughter other than winning the race. Let him race."

When the leper started racing and saw the money, he was startled at first. But soon after he raised his head and continued to race without picking up any money. "It's you I want" he said to himself and to the princess. When he said that, the princess threw even more money at him, but every time, he repeated "it's you I want" keeping his eyes on the finish line.
As the princess slowed down to remove her crown and throw it backways, the leper won the race.

Now after winning the race, the leper looked behind and started picking up the money to put it into his pocket.

Then at an appointed day after the physicians of the kingdom healed his leprosy, he married the princess and was given half the kingdom by the king.

The end.


## Meaning

The King here, represents God. The only child that's a princess represents Jesus, as the only son of the Living God and the people racing to marry him, are you and me everyday.

If you win the princess, you get the king's kingdom. If you win Jesus, you get the Kingdom of God.
It's written, in Matthew 6:33 `Seek first the Kingdom of God above all else, and live righteously, and he will give you everything you need.`

Those who were after the money, procrastinating about catching up with the princess are similar to:

- Let me sin now, I will repent later. Jesus is always there.
Well, there is a day when later, will be after the second coming of Jesus Christ. Thus, `I tell you, now is the time of God's favor, now is the day of salvation.`

- Let me search for money now, I will pray/read the bible tomorrow.
Well, there is a time when everything you will have amassed will not be yours to keep.

Being materially successful doesn't justify us before God. **Romans 3:25** says `For God presented Jesus as the sacrifice for sin. People are made right with God when they believe that Jesus sacrificed his life, shedding his blood`.

An extract of 1 Corinthians 6: 9 says `Do you not know that the unrighteous will not inherit the kingdom of God?[...]` In this story the fate of those who first sought after the material possession got it all taken away from them. Meaning you can't lavish what's for the Kingdom if:
1. You aren't righeous `Hebrews 12:14`
2. You are not worthy of it `Mathiew 22:1-14`.
3. You have decided to choose the world. `Matthew 10: 17-25`, `James 1:27`

**Unless, unless the grace of God falls upon you, `Luke 23: 42-43`<br>**
**The kingdom belongs to those who win the race.**

Those who lost in the contest should have known that money doesn't make them the winners, and having a crown in their hands doesn't make them kings.

The princess giving up her crown is similar to what Jesus did, when He left his throne in heaven and descended here for us. And money being thrown can be seen as things being thrown at us distracting us from the Kingdom.
